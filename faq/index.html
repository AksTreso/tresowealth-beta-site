Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about investing in India through GIFT City IFSC. Quick answers to 40+ common questions for foreign HNIs, EAMs, and Family Offices.

1. GIFT City Fundamentals

What is GIFT City IFSC?
GIFT City (Gujarat International Finance Tec-City) is India's first operational International Financial Services Centre. Located in Gujarat, it's a financial district designed to compete with Singapore, Dubai, and London. IFSC allows foreign investors to access Indian markets with 0% tax, USD-denominated investments, and 48-hour repatriation.
How is GIFT City different from mainland India?
GIFT City offers 0% long-term capital gains tax (vs 20% in mainland), USD-denominated investments (vs INR), 48-hour repatriation (vs 4-6 weeks), and is regulated by IFSCA (vs SEBI). It's designed specifically for foreign investors, not Indian residents.
What is IFSCA and how does it differ from SEBI?
IFSCA (International Financial Services Centres Authority) regulates GIFT City. Unlike SEBI (designed for Indian residents), IFSCA is built for foreign investors. Key differences: 2-week registration (vs 8 weeks), quarterly compliance (vs monthly), English common law framework, and single-window clearance.
What currency are GIFT City investments denominated in?
GIFT City products are denominated in USD, EUR, and AED. This eliminates INR currency risk for foreign investors. If INR falls 10%, your USD investment stays stable. You also save 1-2% annually on hedging costs and get simpler reporting in your base currency.
Is GIFT City safe and regulated?
Yes. GIFT City is regulated by IFSCA, an Indian statutory authority established by Parliament in 2019. It follows English common law, has independent judiciary, and is recognized by major global financial centers. All fund managers must meet IFSCA's stringent due diligence and compliance requirements.

2. Investment Products

What is the difference between Category I, II, and III AIFs?
Category I AIFs invest in startups, venture capital, social impact (high risk, 5-7 year horizon, $100K minimum). Category II AIFs invest in private equity, debt, real estate (medium-high risk, 3-5 years, $50K minimum). Category III AIFs use long-short strategies and hedge funds (medium risk, hedged, 1-3 years, $25K minimum).
What is PMS and how does it differ from mutual funds?
PMS (Portfolio Management Service) gives you a dedicated investment manager for your portfolio. Unlike mutual funds, PMS offers customization (your portfolio, your rules), concentration (can take concentrated positions), transparency (real-time visibility), and tax efficiency (0% LTCG vs 20% in mainland).
What is a Fund of Funds (FoF) and should I consider it?
Fund of Funds invests in 10-15 AIFs/PMS, giving you diversification across managers, strategies, and sectors. Consider it if you want diversification, professional selection, lower minimum (access to $100K+ funds with $25K minimum), and risk management through automatic rebalancing.
What returns can I expect from GIFT City AIFs?
Returns vary by category and fund manager: Category III AIFs target 12-18% annually (market-neutral, lower volatility). Category II AIFs target 15-20% annually (growth-focused, medium volatility). Category I AIFs target 20-30% IRR (early-stage, high volatility). Past performance doesn't guarantee future results.
Are GIFT City AIFs liquid? Can I exit anytime?
Liquidity varies by category: Category III AIFs offer monthly or quarterly liquidity (most liquid). Category II AIFs offer quarterly or semi-annual liquidity (medium liquidity). Category I AIFs have 5-7 year lock-in with no early exit (least liquid). PMS offers monthly liquidity but may have exit loads if redeemed within 1 year.

3. Taxation

How does 0% tax work in GIFT City? Is it legal?
Yes, it's legal and explicitly provided by Indian law. The IFSCA Act 2019 and tax notifications specifically grant 0% tax on long-term capital gains (holdings >1 year), 0% dividend distribution tax, and 0% withholding tax for foreign investors in GIFT City IFSC. This is a policy incentive to attract foreign capital.
Do I need to pay tax in my home country on GIFT City investments?
Depends on your tax residency. UAE residents: 0% personal income tax (check with tax advisor for corporate structures). Saudi residents: 0% personal income tax (check Zakat implications). Other jurisdictions: Check local tax laws. Many countries have DTAA (Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement) with India.
What is DTAA and does it apply to GIFT City investments?
DTAA (Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement) is a treaty between India and other countries to prevent double taxation. Since GIFT City offers 0% tax, DTAA may not be needed for capital gains. However, DTAA can still be relevant for withholding tax on interest (0% in GIFT City), business income taxation, and residency status determination.
Is there any withholding tax on GIFT City investments?
No. GIFT City IFSC has 0% withholding tax on: dividends, interest payments, redemption proceeds, and capital gains. This is a major advantage over mainland India, where withholding tax can be 20% or higher. When you redeem or receive distributions, you get the full amount without Indian tax deduction at source.
Are there any hidden taxes or charges in GIFT City?
No hidden taxes. You pay: Fund management fees (1-2.5% annually, disclosed upfront), and GST on management fees (18% on the fee amount, not on your investment). No: capital gains tax, dividend distribution tax, withholding tax, securities transaction tax, or wealth tax. The total tax drag is essentially 0% on investment returns.

4. Onboarding Process

How long does the onboarding process take?
Total timeline: 2-3 weeks. Week 1: Consultation and fund selection. Week 2: KYC verification and IFSCA registration. Week 3: Fund transfer and portfolio allocation. This is significantly faster than mainland India SEBI routes, which take 6-8 weeks.
What documents are required for KYC?
For individuals: Passport copy (valid), UAE residence visa (if applicable), Emirates ID or national ID, proof of address (utility bill, bank statement), bank account details (for fund transfers), passport-sized photograph, and PAN card exemption form (foreign investors). For corporations: Certificate of incorporation, memorandum & articles of association, board resolution, list of authorized signatories, and ultimate beneficial owner (UBO) declaration.
Do I need an Indian PAN card to invest in GIFT City?
No. Foreign investors (non-Indian residents) are exempt from PAN card requirements for GIFT City IFSC investments. This is a key advantage over mainland India, where PAN is mandatory. You simply need to submit Form 60 (PAN exemption declaration) along with your passport and residence proof.
Can I invest as a corporation or family office?
Yes. GIFT City welcomes corporate and family office investors. Requirements: Company incorporation documents, board resolution authorizing investment, list of authorized signatories, ultimate beneficial owner (UBO) disclosure (for anti-money laundering compliance), and corporate bank account details. Family offices can invest through corporate structures, trusts, or LLCs.
How do I transfer funds to GIFT City?
After IFSCA registration, you'll receive: IFSC bank account details, Swift code, and fund transfer instructions. Transfer methods: Wire transfer from your UAE/international bank (2-3 days), or online banking transfer. Currency: Transfer in USD, EUR, or AED (no INR conversion needed). Your funds are held in a segregated client account at the IFSC banking unit.

5. EAMs & Family Offices

How can DIFC and ADGM EAMs access GIFT City for their clients?
DIFC and ADGM External Asset Managers can access GIFT City through IFSCA registration. Process: Complete IFSCA registration as an investment advisor (2 weeks), establish client onboarding KYC process, select suitable GIFT City AIF products for your clients, and deploy client capital. Benefits: 0% tax (vs 20% mainland), 48-hour repatriation (vs 4-6 weeks), USD-denominated products, and quarterly compliance (vs monthly SEBI).
What compliance requirements apply to EAMs using GIFT City?
IFSCA compliance is designed for foreign EAMs: Quarterly reporting (vs monthly SEBI), English common law framework, single-window clearance, and simplified KYC for foreign clients. EAMs must: Maintain client suitability records, conduct risk assessment for each client, provide quarterly portfolio statements, and report material events to IFSCA. No: Indian registration required, physical presence in India, or complex SEBI compliance.
Can family offices invest directly in GIFT City AIFs?
Yes. Family offices can invest directly: Through corporate structures (LLC, holding company), through trust structures, or as individual HNIs. Minimum investments: $25K (Category III), $50K (Category II), $100K (Category I). Family office benefits: 0% tax on long-term gains, USD-denominated investments (no currency risk), 48-hour repatriation (liquidity), and access to top-tier Indian fund managers.
How does GIFT City compare to other offshore jurisdictions?
GIFT City unique advantages: Direct access to Indian growth (8%+ GDP) vs indirect exposure in other jurisdictions, 0% tax (competitive with Singapore, Dubai), USD-denominated (no INR risk), English common law (like Singapore, Hong Kong), and Indian government backing (sovereign guarantee). Unlike other offshore centers, GIFT City gives you Indian market access with offshore tax benefits.
What client servicing advantages does GIFT City offer EAMs?
GIFT City enables superior client service: Faster onboarding (2 weeks vs 8 weeks SEBI), 48-hour liquidity (vs 4-6 weeks mainland), USD reporting (simpler than INR for foreign clients), quarterly fund performance reports (client-ready), dedicated relationship manager, and custom fund solutions (white-label AIFs). EAMs can offer tax-efficient India allocation (0% vs 20%) and differentiated product access.
