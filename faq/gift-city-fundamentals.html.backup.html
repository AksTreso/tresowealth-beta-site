GIFT City Fundamentals: 10 FAQs Answered

1. What is GIFT City IFSC?
GIFT City (Gujarat International Finance Tec-City) is India's first operational International Financial Services Centre. Located in Gujarat, it's a financial district designed to compete with Singapore, Dubai, and London. IFSC allows foreign investors to access Indian markets with 0% tax, USD-denominated investments, and 48-hour repatriation.
2. How is GIFT City different from mainland India?
GIFT City offers 0% long-term capital gains tax (vs 20% in mainland), USD-denominated investments (vs INR), 48-hour repatriation (vs 4-6 weeks), and is regulated by IFSCA (vs SEBI). It's designed specifically for foreign investors, not Indian residents.
3. What is IFSCA and how does it differ from SEBI?
IFSCA (International Financial Services Centres Authority) regulates GIFT City. Unlike SEBI (designed for Indian residents), IFSCA is built for foreign investors. Key differences: 2-week registration (vs 8 weeks), quarterly compliance (vs monthly), English common law framework, and single-window clearance.
4. What are the tax benefits of investing through GIFT City?
GIFT City offers 0% long-term capital gains tax (holdings >1 year), 0% short-term capital gains tax (Category III AIF), 0% dividend distribution tax, and 0% withholding tax. This means a $100K investment with 15% annual returns over 5 years would be worth $201K in GIFT City vs $155K in mainland India—a 30% difference.
5. Can foreign HNIs invest in GIFT City AIFs?
Yes. GIFT City is specifically designed for foreign HNIs, particularly from GCC countries (UAE, Saudi, Kuwait). Minimum investments start at $25,000 for Category III AIF, $50,000 for Category II, and $100,000 for Category I. No Indian residency or PAN card required.
6. What currency are GIFT City investments denominated in?
GIFT City products are denominated in USD, EUR, and AED. This eliminates INR currency risk for foreign investors. If INR falls 10%, your USD investment stays stable. You also save 1-2% annually on hedging costs and get simpler reporting in your base currency.
7. How long does it take to repatriate funds from GIFT City?
GIFT City offers 48-hour repatriation vs 4-6 weeks in mainland India. This speed is possible because IFSCA has streamlined banking channels and doesn't require RBI approval for outward remittances. Your funds move through the IFSC banking system, not Indian domestic banking.
8. What types of investment products are available in GIFT City?
GIFT City offers AIFs (Alternative Investment Funds) in three categories: Category I (VC, startups, social impact), Category II (PE, debt, real estate), Category III (hedge funds, long-short strategies). It also offers PMS (Portfolio Management Services) and Fund of Funds for diversification across managers.
9. Is GIFT City safe and regulated?
Yes. GIFT City is regulated by IFSCA, an Indian statutory authority established by Parliament in 2019. It follows English common law, has independent judiciary (IFSCA International Financial Services Centres Fund), and is recognized by major global financial centers. All fund managers must meet IFSCA's stringent due diligence and compliance requirements.
10. How do I get started with GIFT City investments?
The process takes 2-3 weeks: 1) Schedule consultation to assess your India allocation target, 2) Complete KYC and IFSCA registration, 3) Select suitable AIF products based on your risk profile, 4) Transfer funds and receive portfolio allocation.
