Onboarding Process FAQ: KYC, Registration, Timeline

1. How long does the onboarding process take?
Total timeline: 2-3 weeks. Week 1: Consultation and fund selection. Week 2: KYC verification and IFSCA registration. Week 3: Fund transfer and portfolio allocation. This is significantly faster than mainland India SEBI routes, which take 6-8 weeks. GIFT City's streamlined IFSCA process and single-window clearance enable faster turnaround.
2. What documents are required for KYC?
For individuals: Passport copy (valid), UAE residence visa (if applicable), Emirates ID or national ID, proof of address (utility bill, bank statement), bank account details (for fund transfers), passport-sized photograph, and PAN card exemption form (foreign investors). For corporations: Certificate of incorporation, memorandum & articles of association, board resolution, list of authorized signatories, and ultimate beneficial owner (UBO) declaration.
3. Do I need an Indian PAN card to invest in GIFT City?
No. Foreign investors (non-Indian residents) are exempt from PAN card requirements for GIFT City IFSC investments. This is a key advantage over mainland India, where PAN is mandatory. You simply need to submit Form 60 (PAN exemption declaration) along with your passport and residence proof. No Indian tax residency or Permanent Account Number required.
4. Can I invest as a corporation or family office?
Yes. GIFT City welcomes corporate and family office investors. Requirements: Company incorporation documents, board resolution authorizing investment, list of authorized signatories, ultimate beneficial owner (UBO) disclosure (for anti-money laundering compliance), and corporate bank account details. Family offices can invest through corporate structures, trusts, or LLCs. Minimum investments remain the same ($25K-$100K depending on AIF category).
5. How do I transfer funds to GIFT City?
After IFSCA registration, you'll receive: IFSC bank account details, Swift code, and fund transfer instructions. Transfer methods: Wire transfer from your UAE/international bank (2-3 days), or online banking transfer. Currency: Transfer in USD, EUR, or AED (no INR conversion needed). Your funds are held in a segregated client account at the IFSC banking unit, separate from fund manager capital. No RBI approval required for inward remittances to GIFT City.
6. What happens during the consultation phase?
Initial consultation (30-60 minutes): Assess your India allocation target (typically 15-20% of portfolio), understand your risk profile (conservative, moderate, aggressive), determine investment horizon (1-3 years for Cat III, 3-5 for Cat II), recommend suitable AIF categories based on your goals, explain fee structure and tax benefits, and answer any questions. Post-consultation: Receive personalized fund recommendations within 48 hours.
7. How does IFSCA registration work?
IFSCA registration is done through your chosen fund manager or TresoWealth. Process: Submit KYC documents, complete risk assessment questionnaire, sign IFSCA investor declaration, receive IFSCA registration number (2 weeks). This is your official investor ID for GIFT City. No need to visit India or any government office. Everything handled remotely. Registration is valid for all future GIFT City investments—no need to re-register for each new fund.
8. Can I invest in multiple AIFs with one registration?
Yes. Your IFSCA registration is valid across all GIFT City IFSC products. Once registered, you can invest in: multiple AIFs (Category I, II, or III), multiple fund managers, PMS services, and Fund of Funds. Each new investment requires: fund-specific application form, fund transfer, and risk disclosure acknowledgment. No additional IFSCA registration needed.
9. What fees should I expect during onboarding?
Onboarding fees: ₹0 (TresoWealth doesn't charge onboarding fees). Fund management fees: 1-2.5% annually (charged by fund manager, disclosed upfront). GST: 18% on management fees (Indian goods & services tax). No: registration fees, account opening charges, or KYC verification fees. Transaction costs: Fund transfer fees (your bank may charge wire transfer fees, typically $25-50). No upfront cost to start investing.
10. What ongoing compliance is required after onboarding?
Minimal ongoing compliance: Annual KYC re-verification (email confirmation, 5 minutes), Receive and review quarterly fund reports, Declare investments in your home country tax filings (if applicable), Update contact/bank details if changed. No: monthly filing requirements, physical presence in India required, or complex tax filings in India (0% tax means simplified compliance). Fund manager handles all Indian regulatory reporting.
