Investment Products FAQ: AIFs, PMS, Fund of Funds

1. What is the difference between Category I, II, and III AIFs?
Category I AIFs invest in startups, venture capital, social impact, and infrastructure. High risk (target early-stage growth), 5-7 year horizon, $100K minimum. Category II AIFs invest in private equity, debt funds, real estate. Medium-high risk (mid-stage growth), 3-5 year horizon, $50K minimum. Category III AIFs use long-short strategies and hedge funds. Medium risk (market-neutral, hedged), 1-3 year horizon, $25K minimum.
2. What is PMS and how does it differ from mutual funds?
PMS (Portfolio Management Service) gives you a dedicated investment manager for your portfolio. Unlike mutual funds, PMS offers customization (your portfolio, your rules), concentration (can take concentrated positions), transparency (real-time visibility), and tax efficiency (0% LTCG vs 20% in mainland). Mutual funds are pooled, one-size-fits-all, with no customization.
3. What is a Fund of Funds (FoF) and should I consider it?
Fund of Funds invests in 10-15 AIFs/PMS, giving you diversification across managers, strategies, and sectors. Consider it if you want: diversification (15 funds in one investment), professional selection (FoF manager does due diligence), lower minimum (access to $100K+ funds with $25K minimum), and risk management (automatic rebalancing, exposure caps). Ideal if you're unsure which fund manager to pick.
4. What is the minimum investment for GIFT City AIFs?
Minimum investments: Category III AIF: $25,000 (hedge funds, long-short strategies, lowest minimum). Category II AIF: $50,000 (private equity, debt, real estate). Category I AIF: $100,000 (VC, startups, infrastructure). PMS minimums start at $100,000. Fund of Funds minimums start at $25,000, giving you access to funds with higher individual minimums.
5. Can I customize my portfolio in GIFT City?
Yes, through PMS (Portfolio Management Services). You can customize based on your preferences: ESG (environmental, social, governance), sector-focused (tech, healthcare, manufacturing), theme-based (consumption, demographics), or risk tolerance (conservative, moderate, aggressive). AIFs have fund manager discretion but less direct customization compared to PMS.
6. How do I select the right AIF category for my portfolio?
Consider your risk tolerance and investment horizon: Category III (1-3 years, medium risk, hedged) if you want market-neutral returns. Category II (3-5 years, medium-high risk) if you want growth with diversification. Category I (5-7 years, high risk) if you want early-stage exposure. Most foreign HNIs start with Category III for stability, then add Category II for diversification. Category I for higher risk tolerance.
7. What returns can I expect from GIFT City AIFs?
Returns vary by category and fund manager: Category III AIFs target 12-18% annually (market-neutral, lower volatility). Category II AIFs target 15-20% annually (growth-focused, medium volatility). Category I AIFs target 20-30% IRR (early-stage, high volatility). Past performance doesn't guarantee future results. Consult a financial advisor to assess suitability for your portfolio.
8. Are GIFT City AIFs liquid? Can I exit anytime?
Liquidity varies by category: Category III AIFs offer monthly or quarterly liquidity (most liquid). Category II AIFs offer quarterly or semi-annual liquidity (medium liquidity). Category I AIFs have 5-7 year lock-in with no early exit (least liquid). PMS offers monthly liquidity but may have exit loads if redeemed within 1 year. Repatriation takes 48 hours after your request is processed.
